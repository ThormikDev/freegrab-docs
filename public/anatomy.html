FreeGrab — Anatomy of the Technique

The interaction decomposed into its functional parts and the relations between them. Every relation raises simple design questions; every answer is a legitimate point in the design space with its own tradeoffs — including answers we have not built.

The argument in one sentence: gaze+pinch is a three-part interaction — gaze selects a target, the hand confirms. FreeGrab adds one new part, the cursor — and every capability the technique gains is literally one of the new part's relations. Target traits (dimensionality, internal structure, multiplicity) don't change the relations; they re-parameterize them.

The relations, question by question

Each relation unfolds into design questions. Every answer lists strengths and weaknesses — there are no rejected answers, only tradeoffs; some answers simply have first-hand evidence attached.

Target traits — the modifiers

The answer to "do the parts interact differently on different targets?" is no — the relations are identical; traits re-parameterize them in three regular ways.

Scope: this prototype covers the unimanual, single-simple-target core; the trait cards point to where hierarchy, multiplicity, and bimanual answers attach.
